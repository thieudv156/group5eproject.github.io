Christmas countdown calendar: what to do at 3 weeks




We've got your back.

Write a master shopping list

Pen a list of all the holiday events you’ll need to stock up for – that includes Christmas Eve, Christmas Day and any Boxing Day soirees or summer barbecues that have crept into the diary. If you’re entertaining more than once (breathe!), this is a good way to find crossover paths: you know, multiple dishes for that haul of cherries? This cherry chocolate pudding cake screams summer, as do these fun and fruity watermelon, cherry and raspberry pops for the kids the next day.

Stock up on all non-perishables

Now’s the time to finish the non-perishable side of the shopping: all those crisps, crackers, sparkling water and cheese-board accompaniments. When it comes to crafting that Insta-worthy cheese board, think seasonally (we’re looking at you again, cherries – but best to add these to the perishables list! More on that later.) And go for what’s on special. Grissini sticks, nuts, dried fruit and rolled-up deli meats (again, a perishable) will make it a board to remember.

Do a baking stocktake

So your Christmas baking Pinterest board is 694 pins deep and your baking books are overflowing with Post-its. We hate to be the bearer of bad news, but it’s unlikely you’re going to get through all those must-bakes in one season. Sorry! Start condensing those lists into one achievable one – we’re not saying ditch that 12-tiered showstopper, but maybe limit it to the one knockout bake and a few supporting roles. Or take it back to the classics with a Christmas pav. As for the rest? Delegate to celebrate!

Team Fruitcake or Pav? Either way, we’ve got you sorted with this collection of Christmas desserts.

Shop for your out-of-town loved ones

If you’re planning that special little Santa parcel for that friend or family member abroad, now’s the time to ship it. If they’re an Aussie away from home, a sentimental best-of box will hit the sweet spot – Tim Tams, Caramello Koalas, Vegemite, Milo, Iced VoVos… And don’t forget the card! Speaking of… Get those holiday cards out the door!
If you’ve opted for snail mail, now’s a great time to mail them. That way, they’ll get the most Christmas decoration mileage at the other end – you know, dressing mantles, blinds, Christmas trees, etc.

Cook up a last-minute batch of freezer meals

The end of year wind-up often means late nights in the office and a social diary that’s so crammed there’s barely time to eat. A little forward planning will go a long way in fending off the take-out monster and potentially safeguard the fragile old holiday immune system. If you’ve already burned through your first batch of frozen meals, set aside a day for a round two cook-up. And, no, freezer meal does not translate to five kilos of spag bol – try our sausage meatballs with chickpeas or vegetarian mushroom and silverbeet lasagna instead.

Pick up a few just-in-case gifts

That awkward moment when a friend unexpectedly hands you a beautifully wrapped parcel and you have nada for them in return can be avoided with a few pre-purchased and pre-wrapped goodies. Boxes of chocolates, scented candles, pre-packaged Christmas puds and fruit mince pies are great to have on hand to save the day. You’re welcome.
